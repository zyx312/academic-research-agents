# Research Grant Audit Preparation Guide

## Purpose

This skill provides a comprehensive framework for preparing for research grant
audits, covering types of audits, documentation requirements, financial record
organization, common audit findings, preparation timelines, and strategies for
working effectively with university finance offices and external auditors.

---

## 1. Types of Research Audits

### Internal University Audits

- **Frequency:** Annual or periodic, conducted by the university's internal audit office
- **Scope:** Compliance with university policies, financial accuracy, effort reporting
- **Severity:** Low to medium; findings lead to corrective actions, not penalties
- **Duration:** 2-4 weeks of active review

### Funder-Initiated Audits

- **Frequency:** Triggered by project completion, risk assessment, or random selection
- **Scope:** Compliance with grant terms, allowability of costs, matching requirements
- **Severity:** Medium to high; findings can result in repayment, debarment
- **Duration:** 4-8 weeks, sometimes longer for complex projects

### Government Compliance Audits

- **Frequency:** Annual for institutions receiving significant government funding
- **Scope:** Organization-wide compliance with regulations (e.g., OMB Uniform Guidance)
- **Severity:** High; affects the institution's ability to receive future funding
- **Duration:** Several months for the institution; individual project review is shorter

### Special or Investigative Audits

- **Trigger:** Whistleblower complaints, suspected fraud, unusual spending patterns
- **Scope:** Narrow but deep; focused on specific allegations
- **Severity:** Very high; can lead to legal consequences
- **Duration:** Variable, often several months

## 2. Documentation Requirements

### Financial Records Checklist

For every expense charged to the grant, maintain:

```
Essential Documents (must have for every transaction):
  [ ] Invoice or receipt (original, not photocopy when possible)
  [ ] Proof of payment (bank record, institutional payment record)
  [ ] Business purpose statement (why this expense was necessary)
  [ ] PI approval (signature or electronic approval)
  [ ] Grant account number charged

Category-Specific Documents:

Personnel:
  [ ] Appointment letters with salary rates
  [ ] Effort certification or time-and-effort reports
  [ ] Payroll records showing amounts charged
  [ ] Justification for any salary above institutional norms
  [ ] Cost-sharing documentation if applicable

Equipment:
  [ ] Procurement records (competitive bids if above threshold)
  [ ] Equipment inventory tag numbers
  [ ] Installation and commissioning records
  [ ] Maintenance logs
  [ ] Disposition records if equipment transferred or disposed

Travel:
  [ ] Travel authorization (pre-approval)
  [ ] Conference agenda or meeting invitation
  [ ] Boarding passes or travel itineraries
  [ ] Hotel receipts with itemized charges
  [ ] Meal receipts (if not using per diem)
  [ ] Trip report connecting travel to project objectives

Subcontracts:
  [ ] Executed subcontract agreement
  [ ] Invoices from subcontractor
  [ ] Progress reports from subcontractor
  [ ] Evidence of subcontractor deliverables
  [ ] Monitoring records

Materials and Supplies:
  [ ] Purchase orders or requisitions
  [ ] Delivery receipts
  [ ] Inventory records for high-value items
  [ ] Connection to specific research activities
```

### Non-Financial Records

```
Project Documentation:
  [ ] Original proposal (approved version)
  [ ] Award notice and terms/conditions
  [ ] Budget (original and any approved modifications)
  [ ] Progress reports (midterm and annual)
  [ ] Final report
  [ ] Correspondence with funder about changes

Research Records:
  [ ] Lab notebooks or research logs
  [ ] Data management records
  [ ] IRB/ethics approval documents
  [ ] Publication list
  [ ] Patent filings

Personnel Records:
  [ ] Student and postdoc appointment letters
  [ ] Effort certifications
  [ ] Training completion records
  [ ] Conflict of interest disclosures
```

### Digital Record Keeping Best Practices

1. **Scan all paper receipts** immediately and store digitally
2. **Use consistent naming conventions:** Date_Vendor_Description_Amount
3. **Back up regularly** to university-approved cloud storage
4. **Maintain version control** for budget documents
5. **Cross-reference** each digital record with the accounting system transaction ID
6. **Retain records** for the required period (typically 3-7 years post-closeout)

## 4. Common Audit Findings

### High-Frequency Findings

| Finding | Description | Prevention |
|---------|------------|-----------|
| Missing receipts | No documentation for claimed expenses | Submit receipts within 30 days; scan immediately |
| Inadequate business purpose | Expense not clearly connected to project | Write specific justification for every expense |
| Effort reporting gaps | Time and effort not properly documented | Complete effort certifications on schedule |
| Cost misallocation | Expenses charged to wrong grant | Review monthly statements; correct within 90 days |
| Procurement violations | Not following competitive bidding rules | Check thresholds before purchasing |
| Late cost transfers | Moving expenses between accounts after deadline | Transfer within 90 days; document reason |
| Unallowable costs | Charging prohibited expenses to grant | Review allowability before purchasing |
| Missing pre-approvals | Travel or purchases without prior authorization | Get approvals before committing funds |

### Moderate-Risk Findings

| Finding | Description | Prevention |
|---------|------------|-----------|
| Budget deviation without approval | Spending exceeds approved category by > threshold | Monitor monthly; request rebudgeting proactively |
| Subcontractor monitoring gaps | No evidence of reviewing subcontractor performance | Request quarterly progress reports |
| Equipment inventory discrepancies | Equipment cannot be located or accounted for | Annual physical inventory check |
| Cost sharing shortfalls | Promised matching not fully documented | Track cost sharing in parallel with grant spending |
| Carryforward without approval | Moving unspent funds to next year without request | Submit carryforward request before year end |

## 5. Audit Preparation Timeline

### When You Receive Audit Notification

```
Immediately (Day 1-3):
  - Notify university research administration office
  - Identify all team members who need to participate
  - Locate the grant file and verify completeness
  - Review the scope of the audit (what period, what aspects)

Week 1:
  - Gather all financial records for the audit period
  - Reconcile your records against the university's financial system
  - Identify any gaps in documentation
  - Begin preparing explanations for unusual transactions

Week 2:
  - Organize physical and digital files in the recommended structure
  - Prepare a summary of project activities and outcomes
  - Brief team members on their roles during the audit
  - Prepare a list of anticipated questions and answers

Week 3:
  - Conduct a self-audit using the common findings checklist
  - Resolve any discoverable issues before auditors arrive
  - Prepare a clean workspace for auditor use
  - Have all files accessible and clearly labeled

Week 4+:
  - Auditors on site (or reviewing remotely)
  - Respond to auditor requests promptly (within 24-48 hours)
  - Document all interactions with auditors
  - Do not volunteer information not requested
  - Be honest and transparent about any issues
```

### Ongoing Audit Readiness (Proactive)

Instead of scrambling when audit notice arrives, maintain audit readiness
throughout the project:

```
Monthly:
  - Reconcile expenses against university statements
  - File all receipts and documentation
  - Update budget tracking spreadsheet
  - Review effort certifications

Quarterly:
  - Review subcontractor deliverables and invoices
  - Check equipment inventory
  - Verify cost sharing documentation
  - Assess compliance with grant terms

Annually:
  - Conduct self-audit using the common findings checklist
  - Update project files with progress reports
  - Review and update budget projections
  - Ensure all personnel effort certifications are current
```

## 6. Working with Auditors

### Dos and Don'ts

**Do:**
- Be professional, cooperative, and responsive
- Provide requested documents promptly
- Answer questions truthfully and concisely
- Keep records of all documents provided
- Ask for clarification if a request is unclear
- Involve the university's research administration office early
- Correct any errors found during the audit proactively

**Do Not:**
- Volunteer information beyond what is asked
- Speculate about answers you are unsure of (say "I will check and get back to you")
- Alter or backdate any documents
- Delay responses or be evasive
- Argue with auditors about their findings during the audit
- Discuss audit details with people outside the project team
- Assume a finding means wrongdoing (many are procedural)

### Responding to Audit Findings

```
Step 1: Review the finding carefully
  - Understand exactly what the auditor is asserting
  - Determine if the finding is factually accurate

Step 2: Prepare a response
  - If the finding is valid: acknowledge, explain the cause, describe
    corrective action taken and planned
  - If the finding is incorrect: provide documentation that refutes it
  - If the finding is partially correct: acknowledge the valid parts,
    clarify the incorrect parts with evidence

Step 3: Implement corrective actions
  - Make changes to prevent recurrence
  - Document the new procedures
  - Train team members on updated processes

Step 4: Follow up
  - Ensure corrective actions are completed on schedule
  - Monitor for recurrence
  - Update policies and procedures documentation
```

## 7. Working with University Finance Office

### Establishing a Good Relationship

1. **Meet at project start:** Introduce yourself and the project
2. **Learn the system:** Understand the university's financial system and codes
3. **Communicate proactively:** Inform finance of upcoming large purchases
4. **Submit on time:** Do not delay expense reports or approvals
5. **Ask before spending:** Check allowability before committing funds
6. **Respond promptly** to finance office inquiries

### Key Questions to Ask Finance at Project Start

- What is the account or cost center number for this grant?
- What are the procurement thresholds and bidding requirements?
- How do I request rebudgeting?
- What is the travel pre-approval process?
- When are effort certifications due?
- What is the deadline for end-of-year spending?
- How long must I retain financial records?
- Who is my assigned grants accountant?

## 8. Self-Audit Checklist

Conduct this review annually and before any anticipated audit:

### Financial Compliance
- [ ] All expenses have receipts or invoices on file
- [ ] Business purpose is documented for every expense
- [ ] PI approval is on record for all transactions
- [ ] Expenses are charged to the correct grant account
- [ ] Cost transfers are completed within the required timeframe
- [ ] Procurement procedures were followed for all purchases

### Effort and Personnel
- [ ] Effort certifications are current and signed
- [ ] Personnel appointments align with grant-funded positions
- [ ] Salary rates match institutional pay scales
- [ ] Cost sharing is documented and on track

### Equipment and Inventory
- [ ] Equipment inventory is up to date
- [ ] All tagged equipment can be physically located
- [ ] Equipment is being used for grant-related purposes
- [ ] Disposition records exist for any removed equipment

### Reporting and Administration
- [ ] Progress reports submitted on time
- [ ] Budget modifications properly requested and documented
- [ ] Subcontractor monitoring records are complete
- [ ] IRB/ethics approvals are current and on file

```
Help me write a formal response to an audit finding.

Finding: [DESCRIBE THE AUDITOR'S FINDING]
Finding category: [financial / compliance / effort / documentation]

Our assessment: [agree / partially agree / disagree]

Supporting documentation available:
[LIST DOCUMENTS YOU CAN PROVIDE]

Write a formal response that:
1. Acknowledges the finding professionally
2. Provides our position with supporting evidence
3. Describes the root cause (if we agree with the finding)
4. Outlines specific corrective actions with deadlines
5. Explains how we will prevent recurrence
6. Maintains a cooperative and constructive tone
```
