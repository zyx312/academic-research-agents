# Research Grant Budget Management Guide

## Purpose

This skill provides a comprehensive framework for managing research grant budgets,
covering budget categories, allocation strategies, spending timelines, rebudgeting
procedures, compliance requirements, and audit preparation. It applies to NSFC,
provincial funds, ministry projects, industry contracts, and international grants.

---

## 1. Budget Categories

### Standard Research Budget Categories

| Category | Description | Typical % | Examples |
|----------|-----------|-----------|---------|
| Personnel | Salaries, stipends, benefits | 30-50% | RA/TA stipends, postdoc salary, PI summer salary |
| Equipment | Durable goods above threshold | 10-25% | Servers, sensors, test equipment, vehicles |
| Travel | Domestic and international | 5-15% | Conference attendance, field work, collaboration visits |
| Materials and supplies | Consumable items | 5-15% | Computing cloud credits, sensor components, chemicals |
| Publication fees | Open access and page charges | 2-5% | Journal APCs, color figure charges |
| Subcontracts | Work by external partners | 0-30% | Collaborating institutions, testing services |
| Indirect costs | University overhead | 8-15% | Facilities, administrative support (rate set by institution) |
| Other | Miscellaneous allowable costs | 0-5% | Software licenses, data storage, participant compensation |

### Category-Specific Guidelines

**Personnel:**
- Largest budget item in most research grants
- Graduate RA stipends are often the primary personnel cost
- Track effort commitments (percentage of time) carefully
- Ensure personnel costs align with institutional salary scales
- Document any cost sharing or matching contributions

**Equipment:**
- Usually defined as items costing above a threshold (often 5,000-10,000 CNY/USD)
- Equipment purchases are often front-loaded (Year 1)
- Requires competitive bidding above certain thresholds
- Maintain inventory records with serial numbers and locations
- Equipment belongs to the institution, not the PI, after the project

**Travel:**
- Conference travel should align with project dissemination goals
- Domestic travel for fieldwork or collaboration meetings
- International travel may require pre-approval
- Keep all receipts, boarding passes, and conference registrations
- Per diem rates must follow institutional policies

**Materials and Supplies:**
- Cloud computing credits (AWS, Alibaba Cloud, etc.)
- Consumable experimental materials
- Small equipment items below the capital threshold
- Software licenses (annual or perpetual)

## 2. Budget Allocation Strategies

### Front-Loading vs Even Distribution

```
Front-Loaded Budget (equipment-heavy projects):
  Year 1: 45% -- equipment purchases, setup costs
  Year 2: 35% -- primary research activities
  Year 3: 20% -- analysis, writing, dissemination

Even Distribution (personnel-heavy projects):
  Year 1: 33% -- personnel, initial experiments
  Year 2: 34% -- core research, conferences
  Year 3: 33% -- writing, final experiments

Back-Loaded Budget (avoid if possible):
  Year 1: 20% -- planning, hiring
  Year 2: 30% -- early experiments
  Year 3: 50% -- rushed spending (raises audit flags)
```

### Monthly Cash Flow Planning

Create a monthly spending plan at the start of the project:

```
Month    Personnel  Equipment  Travel  Materials  Other    Total
Jan      25,000     50,000     0       5,000      2,000    82,000
Feb      25,000     0          0       5,000      1,000    31,000
Mar      25,000     0          8,000   5,000      1,000    39,000
...
Dec      25,000     0          0       3,000      1,000    29,000
-------------------------------------------------------------------
Annual   300,000    80,000     40,000  50,000     15,000   485,000
```

### Spending Velocity Monitoring

Track spending rate monthly and compare against plan:

- **Healthy:** Within 10% of expected cumulative spending
- **Under-spending warning:** More than 20% below expected at midpoint
- **Over-spending warning:** More than 15% above expected at any point
- **Critical:** Projected to exhaust funds before project end

## 3. Spending Timeline Best Practices

### Year 1 Priorities

- Purchase major equipment early (procurement takes time)
- Hire research assistants and establish stipend schedules
- Set up computing infrastructure and software licenses
- Attend one conference to present project kickoff / related work
- Establish subcontracts and collaboration agreements

### Year 2 Priorities

- Primary research expenditures (experiments, data collection)
- Conference travel for presenting initial results
- Materials and consumables for core experiments
- Publication fees for first papers
- Equipment maintenance and upgrades if needed

### Year 3 Priorities (Final Year)

- Publication fees for remaining papers
- Conference travel for final results presentation
- Final data archiving and documentation costs
- Student completion costs (thesis printing, defense expenses)
- Avoid large equipment purchases (raises questions)

### End-of-Grant Spending Deadlines

- Most grants have a hard deadline for expenditures
- Final 3 months: only committed obligations (no new purchases)
- Final 1 month: only completing existing orders
- Check with grants office for specific cut-off dates
- Plan to have all spending complete 1-2 months before deadline

## 4. Rebudgeting Rules

### When Rebudgeting Is Needed

- Spending in a category will exceed the approved amount by more than
  the allowed variance (typically 10-20%)
- A new expense category not in the original budget is needed
- Equipment costs differ significantly from original estimates
- Personnel changes require budget redistribution
- Project scope adjustments affect spending patterns

### Rebudgeting Process

```
Step 1: Identify the need
  - Which categories need adjustment?
  - How much needs to move and in which direction?
  - What is the justification?

Step 2: Check allowable limits
  - Many funders allow 10-20% rebudgeting without formal approval
  - Changes exceeding the threshold require written approval
  - Some categories (equipment, indirect) may be fixed

Step 3: Prepare documentation
  - Written justification for each change
  - Revised budget table showing before/after
  - Impact statement on project activities

Step 4: Submit for approval
  - Internal: university grants office
  - External: funding agency (if exceeds automatic threshold)
  - Allow 4-8 weeks for approval processing

Step 5: Update records
  - Revise internal budget tracking spreadsheet
  - Notify team members of new spending limits
  - File approval documentation for audit trail
```

### Common Rebudgeting Scenarios

| Scenario | Action | Approval Needed? |
|----------|--------|-----------------|
| RA quits, need to hire replacement | Adjust personnel timing | Usually no |
| Equipment costs 15% more than estimated | Move from materials to equipment | Depends on amount |
| Conference cancelled, redirect travel funds | Move travel to other categories | Usually auto-allowed |
| New collaboration requires subcontract | Add new category | Yes, formal approval |
| Unspent Year 1 funds carry to Year 2 | Carry-forward request | Check funder policy |

## 5. Compliance Essentials

### Allowable vs Unallowable Costs

| Allowable | Unallowable |
|-----------|-------------|
| Personnel working on the project | Entertainment and social events |
| Equipment for research use | Personal expenses of any kind |
| Conference registration and travel | Alcohol |
| Journal publication fees | Fines and penalties |
| Research materials and supplies | Lobbying or political activities |
| Data storage and computing | General office furniture (usually) |
| Participant compensation (IRB approved) | Construction or renovation |

### Documentation Requirements for Every Expense

1. **Invoice or receipt** with vendor name, date, amount, and items
2. **Proof of payment** (bank transfer record, credit card statement)
3. **Business purpose** statement connecting expense to project
4. **Approval** by PI or authorized signatory
5. **Procurement documentation** (quotes, bids) for purchases above threshold
6. **Travel documentation** (itinerary, conference agenda, boarding passes)

### Common Compliance Violations

- Charging personal expenses to the grant
- Splitting purchases to avoid procurement thresholds
- Charging travel not related to the project
- Missing or incomplete receipts
- Charging costs after the grant period ends
- Failing to report cost sharing commitments
- Using grant funds for a different project (cross-charging)

## 6. Tips for Maximizing Research Value

### Stretch Your Budget

1. **Leverage institutional resources:** Use shared equipment, library
   subscriptions, and computing clusters included in overhead
2. **Apply for travel grants:** Many conferences offer student travel support
3. **Use open-source tools:** Reduce software licensing costs
4. **Cloud computing credits:** Apply for academic research credits from
   AWS, Google Cloud, Azure, Alibaba Cloud
5. **Collaborate strategically:** Share data collection or equipment costs
   with collaborators on related grants
6. **Bulk purchasing:** Buy consumables in larger quantities when cost-effective
7. **Open access options:** Use preprint servers and green OA to reduce
   publication costs
8. **Student conference volunteering:** Some conferences waive registration
   for student volunteers

### Avoid Wasting Budget

1. Do not buy equipment that will sit unused
2. Do not attend conferences without presenting or clear networking goals
3. Do not hire personnel without clear tasks defined
4. Do not purchase software licenses before confirming the tool meets needs
5. Do not wait until the last month to spend remaining funds

## 7. Financial Monitoring Tools

### Spreadsheet-Based Tracking

Minimum viable budget tracking system:

```
Columns:
  Date | Category | Description | Amount | Vendor | Receipt # |
  Approved By | Grant Account # | Running Balance

Tabs:
  - Transactions (all expenses logged)
  - Summary (pivot by category, monthly totals)
  - Budget vs Actual (comparison with approved budget)
  - Projections (estimated future spending)
```

### Monthly Review Checklist

- [ ] Reconcile transactions with university financial system
- [ ] Compare spending to monthly cash flow plan
- [ ] Check for any unrecorded expenses
- [ ] Verify all receipts are filed
- [ ] Review upcoming commitments (travel booked, orders pending)
- [ ] Update spending projections for remaining period
- [ ] Flag any categories approaching or exceeding limits
- [ ] Communicate status to co-PIs if applicable

## 8. Working with University Finance

### Key Contacts

- **Grants accountant:** Processes transactions, provides financial reports
- **Procurement officer:** Handles equipment purchases and bidding
- **Research administrator:** Manages grant compliance and reporting
- **Post-award coordinator:** Assists with budget modifications

### Best Practices for PI-Finance Collaboration

1. Meet with grants accountant at project start to understand procedures
2. Request monthly financial statements from the university system
3. Submit expense reimbursements promptly (within 30 days)
4. Communicate travel plans and equipment needs in advance
5. Ask questions early rather than making assumptions about allowability
6. Keep the research administrator informed of any project changes
7. Build a good working relationship (responsive, organized, respectful)
