# Teaching Reform Skill

## Overview

This skill supports faculty in developing teaching reform projects (教改项目), engaging with the Scholarship of Teaching and Learning (SoTL), writing teaching innovation proposals, measuring teaching effectiveness, and publishing teaching research. It bridges Chinese higher education teaching reform practices with international SoTL frameworks.

## Types of Teaching Reform Projects (教改项目)

### National Level (国家级)
- **Ministry of Education Projects** (教育部教改项目): Highest prestige, typically 2-3 year duration
- **New Engineering/New Liberal Arts** (新工科/新文科): Discipline-specific reform initiatives
- **First-Class Course Construction** (一流课程建设): National-level course designation (线上、线下、混合、虚拟仿真、社会实践)
- **Teaching Achievement Awards** (教学成果奖): Recognition of sustained teaching innovations with demonstrated impact

### Provincial Level (省级)
- Provincial education department projects (省教育厅教改项目)
- Provincial quality courses (省级精品课程)
- Provincial teaching team designations
- Provincial experimental teaching demonstration centers

### Institutional Level (校级)
- Internal teaching reform grants
- Course construction projects
- Teaching method innovation projects
- Curriculum reform projects
- Textbook development projects (教材建设)

### Common Focus Areas
- Curriculum system reform (课程体系改革)
- Teaching method innovation (教学方法创新)
- Assessment reform (考核方式改革)
- Industry-education integration (产教融合)
- Course ideology integration (课程思政)
- Experimental/practical teaching reform (实验实践教学改革)
- Online and blended learning (线上线下混合教学)
- Innovation and entrepreneurship education (创新创业教育)

## SoTL Framework (Scholarship of Teaching and Learning)

### Definition and Principles

SoTL is systematic inquiry into student learning that advances the practice of teaching by making findings public. It goes beyond being an excellent teacher by treating teaching as a subject of scholarly investigation.

Peter Felten's 5 Principles of Good Practice in SoTL:
1. **Inquiry focused on student learning**: The central question must be about how students learn
2. **Grounded in context**: Research acknowledges the specific teaching and learning environment
3. **Methodologically sound**: Uses appropriate research methods (qualitative, quantitative, or mixed)
4. **Conducted in partnership with students**: Students are collaborators, not just subjects
5. **Appropriately public**: Results are shared for peer review and use by others

### SoTL vs. Good Teaching vs. Scholarly Teaching

| Aspect | Good Teaching | Scholarly Teaching | SoTL |
| --- | --- | --- | --- |
| Uses literature | Sometimes | Yes | Yes |
| Collects evidence | Informally | Yes | Systematically |
| Peer-reviewed | No | Sometimes | Yes |
| Published | No | No | Yes |
| Transferable | Limited | Moderate | High |

### SoTL Research Questions

Strong SoTL questions are:
- Focused on student learning (not just teaching methods)
- Grounded in prior literature
- Investigable with available methods and data
- Relevant beyond a single classroom

Examples:
- "How does structured peer review affect the quality of student writing in [course]?"
- "What is the impact of spaced retrieval practice on long-term retention in [subject]?"
- "How do students experience and navigate the transition from procedural to conceptual understanding in [topic]?"

### SoTL Research Methods

**Quantitative approaches:**
- Pre/post assessments (concept inventories, skills tests)
- Quasi-experimental designs (comparison between sections)
- Survey research (validated instruments like MSLQ, CLASS, SALG)
- Learning analytics (LMS data, time-on-task)

**Qualitative approaches:**
- Think-aloud protocols
- Student interviews and focus groups
- Reflective journals analysis
- Classroom observation protocols

**Mixed methods:**
- Sequential explanatory: Quantitative data collection followed by qualitative exploration
- Concurrent triangulation: Both types collected simultaneously

## Proposal Structure for Teaching Reform Grants

### Standard Chinese Teaching Reform Proposal (教改项目申请书)

1. **Project Title** (项目名称): Concise, specific, action-oriented
2. **Project Team** (项目组成员): PI, co-PIs, team members with roles
3. **Background and Rationale** (研究背景与意义)
   - Current problems in teaching this subject
   - Gap between current practice and desired outcomes
   - Policy alignment (national education priorities)
   - Literature review of relevant innovations
4. **Research Objectives** (研究目标)
   - Specific, measurable outcomes
   - Expected innovations and breakthroughs
5. **Reform Content and Methods** (改革内容与方法)
   - Detailed description of what will change
   - How the innovation will be implemented
   - Timeline with milestones
6. **Expected Outcomes** (预期成果)
   - Deliverables (course materials, publications, reports)
   - Student learning improvements (with metrics)
   - Scalability and transferability plans
7. **Team Qualifications** (团队基础)
   - Prior teaching achievements
   - Relevant research or reform experience
   - Complementary expertise of team members
8. **Budget** (经费预算)
   - Materials and resources
   - Travel (conferences, benchmarking visits)
   - Student assistants
   - Publication and dissemination costs

### International Teaching Grant Proposal

For grants from teaching centers, foundations, or disciplinary societies:

1. **Abstract** (250 words)
2. **Introduction and Context**
3. **Literature Review**
4. **Research Questions or Innovation Goals**
5. **Methods and Implementation Plan**
6. **Assessment and Evaluation Plan**
7. **Timeline and Milestones**
8. **Dissemination Plan**
9. **Budget and Justification**
10. **References**
11. **IRB/Ethics Considerations** (if involving human subjects research)

## Evidence-Based Teaching Innovations

### High-Impact Practices
- **Flipped Classroom**: Pre-class content delivery, in-class active learning
- **Team-Based Learning (TBL)**: Permanent teams, readiness assurance, application exercises
- **Process-Oriented Guided Inquiry Learning (POGIL)**: Student teams work through guided activities
- **Specification Grading**: Pass/fail on clear specifications, emphasizing mastery
- **Retrieval Practice**: Frequent low-stakes testing to strengthen memory
- **Interleaving**: Mixing different problem types rather than blocking by topic

### Technology-Enabled Innovations
- Virtual simulation labs (虚拟仿真实验)
- AI-assisted personalized learning paths
- Learning analytics-driven early warning systems
- Massive Open Online Courses (MOOCs) for blended learning
- VR/AR for experiential learning
- Digital portfolios for competency tracking

### Pedagogical Approaches with Strong Evidence
- **Active learning**: Meta-analyses show consistent improvement over traditional lecture
- **Spaced practice**: Distributing study over time improves long-term retention
- **Elaborative interrogation**: Asking students "why" and "how" deepens understanding
- **Peer instruction**: Students teaching each other clarifies misconceptions
- **Worked examples**: Step-by-step solutions followed by gradually faded scaffolding

## Measuring Teaching Effectiveness

### Multiple Data Sources (Triangulation)

1. **Student Learning Outcomes**
   - Pre/post knowledge assessments
   - Course grade distributions (compared to similar sections)
   - DFW (D grade, Fail, Withdraw) rates
   - Performance on standardized concept inventories
   - Long-term retention (assessed in subsequent courses)

2. **Student Perception Data**
   - End-of-course evaluations (use with caution: known biases)
   - Mid-semester feedback (more actionable)
   - Focus groups or interviews
   - Alumni surveys (retrospective value assessment)

3. **Peer and Expert Observation**
   - Classroom observation protocols (e.g., COPUS, RTOP)
   - Teaching portfolio review
   - Peer review of course materials
   - External evaluator reports

4. **Self-Assessment and Reflection**
   - Teaching journals and reflective practice
   - Video review of own teaching
   - Analysis of student work samples
   - Tracking of innovations and their outcomes

### Avoiding Pitfalls in Measurement
- Do not rely solely on student evaluations (biased by gender, race, discipline)
- Use control groups or historical comparisons when possible
- Account for student demographics and prior preparation
- Measure learning gains, not just final achievement
- Collect data systematically from the start of the intervention

- Lilly Conferences on Teaching and Learning

### Writing a SoTL Paper

Structure for an empirical SoTL article:
1. **Introduction**: The teaching problem and why it matters
2. **Literature Review**: What is known, what gap exists
3. **Context**: Description of institution, course, students
4. **Methods**: Study design, participants, instruments, analysis approach
5. **Results**: What the data show (with appropriate statistics or themes)
6. **Discussion**: Interpretation, limitations, implications
7. **Conclusions**: Key takeaways and recommendations for practice

Common reviewer criticisms to preempt:
- "This is just describing what you did" (must include evidence of impact)
- "Small sample size" (acknowledge limitation, use appropriate methods)
- "No theoretical framework" (ground in learning theory)
- "Lacks rigor" (describe methods thoroughly, address validity threats)

## Building a Teaching Portfolio

### Components
- Teaching philosophy statement (1-2 pages)
- Summary of courses taught with enrollments
- Evidence of teaching effectiveness (multiple data sources)
- Sample syllabi, assignments, and course materials
- Teaching innovations with evidence of impact
- Teaching awards and recognitions
- Mentorship and advising record
- Teaching-related publications and presentations
- Professional development activities
- Future teaching goals

### Teaching Philosophy Statement
- Articulate your beliefs about learning and teaching
- Connect beliefs to specific teaching practices
- Provide concrete examples and evidence
- Show growth and evolution in your thinking
- Keep it authentic and discipline-specific

## Prompt Templates

### Draft a Teaching Reform Proposal (教改项目申请书)

```
I need to write a teaching reform proposal:
- Project type: [national/provincial/institutional level]
- Course or program: [title, discipline, level]
- Problem to address: [specific teaching/learning challenge]
- Proposed innovation: [what you plan to change]
- Team members: [names and expertise]
- Duration: [timeline]
- Funding amount: [if known]

Please draft a complete proposal that:
1. Frames the problem with evidence (student data, literature)
2. Connects to national education policy priorities (if Chinese context)
3. Describes the innovation in detail with implementation steps
4. Includes a rigorous evaluation plan with specific metrics
5. Lists concrete deliverables and expected outcomes
6. Provides a realistic timeline with milestones
7. Demonstrates the team's qualifications
8. Addresses scalability and sustainability
```

### Design a SoTL Study

```
I want to conduct a SoTL study in my course:
- Course: [title, level, enrollment]
- Teaching innovation: [what I changed or plan to change]
- Research question: [what I want to learn about student learning]
- Available data sources: [exam scores, surveys, LMS data, etc.]
- Constraints: [time, resources, IRB requirements]

Help me design a rigorous study:
1. Refine the research question for clarity and focus
2. Suggest appropriate research methodology
3. Recommend validated instruments or assessment tools
4. Outline a data collection and analysis plan
5. Identify potential threats to validity and how to address them
6. Draft an IRB/ethics application outline
7. Suggest target journals for publication
```

### Write a Teaching Philosophy Statement

```
Help me write a teaching philosophy statement:
- Discipline: [field]
- Teaching experience: [years, types of courses]
- Core beliefs about learning: [brief description]
- Signature teaching methods: [what I do that is distinctive]
- Evidence of impact: [student outcomes, feedback, awards]
- Purpose: [tenure file, job application, award nomination]

The statement should:
1. Open with a compelling framing of my approach
2. Connect beliefs to concrete practices with examples
3. Include evidence of effectiveness
4. Show awareness of inclusive and equitable teaching
5. Demonstrate growth and reflection
6. Be [N] pages, in a scholarly but personal voice
```

### Evaluate a Teaching Innovation

```
I implemented the following teaching innovation:
- Innovation: [description of what was changed]
- Course context: [course, enrollment, semester]
- Data collected: [exam scores, survey results, student work, etc.]

Please help me:
1. Organize the data into a coherent analysis framework
2. Identify appropriate statistical or qualitative analysis methods
3. Interpret the results in context
4. Compare findings to relevant literature
5. Acknowledge limitations honestly
6. Draft findings and discussion sections suitable for publication
7. Suggest how to strengthen the evidence in future iterations
```
