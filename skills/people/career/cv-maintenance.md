# CV Maintenance Skill

## Overview

This skill guides the creation, maintenance, and strategic use of academic CVs. It covers the full CV structure for professors, formatting conventions, tailoring for different purposes, and managing your online scholarly presence. An up-to-date, well-organized CV is the foundation of academic career management.

## Academic CV vs. Resume vs. Biosketch

### Academic CV (Curriculum Vitae)
- **Length**: No limit; grows throughout career (10-30+ pages for senior faculty)
- **Content**: Comprehensive record of all scholarly activities
- **Purpose**: Job applications, promotion dossiers, award nominations
- **Updates**: Continuously maintained as new achievements occur

### Resume
- **Length**: 1-2 pages
- **Content**: Selected highlights tailored to a specific opportunity
- **Purpose**: Industry positions, consulting, non-academic applications
- **Updates**: Created fresh for each opportunity from CV material

### Biosketch / Bio
- **Length**: 2-5 pages (format varies by agency)
- **Content**: Selected, formatted per specific agency requirements
- **Purpose**: Grant applications (NSF, NIH, NSFC, ERC)
- **Updates**: Created per application from CV material
- **Examples**: NSF Biosketch (3 pages), NIH Biosketch (5 pages), NSFC resume (国家自然科学基金简历)

## Full Academic CV Structure

### 1. Header and Contact Information
- Full name (with Chinese characters if applicable: 张玉新 / Yuxin Zhang)
- Current title and department
- University and full mailing address
- Email address (institutional preferred)
- Phone number
- Personal academic website URL
- ORCID identifier
- Google Scholar profile URL

### 2. Research Interests
- 3-5 keyword areas describing your research focus
- Written as phrases, not sentences
- Update as your research evolves
- Balance breadth (for search committees) with specificity (for experts)

### 3. Education
- List in reverse chronological order
- Include: Degree, Field, Institution, Year, Advisor (optional for PhD)
- Dissertation title (for PhD)
- Do NOT include GPA (unnecessary for faculty CVs)

### 4. Academic Appointments
- Current and past positions in reverse chronological order
- Include: Title, Department, Institution, Dates (start-end or start-present)
- Include visiting positions, postdoctoral appointments
- Clearly label current position

### 5. Publications

**Organization options:**
- By type (journal articles, conference papers, book chapters, books)
- By topic area (for interdisciplinary researchers)
- Numbered reverse chronologically (most common)

**Journal Articles:**
- Use consistent citation format throughout
- Bold or underline your name
- Mark corresponding author with asterisk (*)
- Include DOI or URL
- Note impact factor or JCR ranking if appropriate for your field
- Separate published from under review / in press

**Conference Papers:**
- Distinguish peer-reviewed full papers from abstracts and posters
- Include acceptance rate for competitive venues (if known)

**Other publication types:**
- Book chapters
- Books (authored or edited)
- Technical reports
- Patents
- Working papers or preprints (labeled clearly)

### 6. Grants and Funding
- List in reverse chronological order
- Include: Title, Funding Agency, Amount, Duration, Role (PI/Co-PI)
- Separate active from completed grants
- Include both funded and under-review proposals (labeled clearly)
- For NSFC: include project number (项目批准号)

### 7. Awards and Honors
- Reverse chronological order
- Include: Award name, Granting organization, Year
- Include best paper awards, teaching awards, fellowships
- Note selectivity if relevant ("1 of 3 awarded nationally")

### 8. Teaching Experience
- Courses taught with institution, level, enrollment, semester/year
- Distinguish between courses developed vs. inherited
- Include teaching innovations and curriculum development
- List graduate courses separately from undergraduate

### 9. Student Supervision
- **Doctoral students**: Name, thesis title, dates, current position
- **Master's students**: Name, thesis title, dates, current position
- **Postdoctoral researchers**: Name, dates, current position
- **Undergraduate researchers**: Summary count or selected names
- Organize as: Current, Completed (with placement information)
- Committee memberships (external examiner, thesis committee)

### 10. Service
**Professional service:**
- Journal editorial board memberships
- Conference organizing committee roles
- Peer review activity (journals reviewed for, review count)
- Professional society leadership positions
- Grant review panel service

**Institutional service:**
- Department, college, and university committee memberships
- Administrative roles
- Faculty governance

**Community/Public service:**
- Public lectures, media appearances
- Community engagement activities
- K-12 outreach
- Policy advisory roles

### 11. Invited Talks and Presentations
- Separate invited talks from contributed presentations
- Include: Title, Event/Institution, Location, Date
- List keynotes and plenaries prominently

### 12. Professional Development
- Workshops and training attended
- Certifications (if relevant)
- Sabbaticals and research visits

### 13. Skills and Languages (Optional)
- Programming languages, software, laboratory techniques
- Language proficiencies
- Professional certifications

## Formatting Conventions

### Universal Standards
- **Font**: Professional serif or sans-serif (Times New Roman, Garamond, Calibri), 11-12pt
- **Margins**: 1 inch (2.54 cm) on all sides
- **Consistency**: Same format for all entries of the same type
- **Dates**: Consistent format throughout (e.g., "2020-present" or "Sept. 2020 - present")
- **Page numbers**: Include on every page (especially for long CVs)
- **Header**: Name on every page after the first

### Field-Specific Norms
- **STEM**: Publications section is the longest and most detailed; include citation counts selectively
- **Humanities**: Publications and teaching sections are both prominent; include book reviews
- **Social Sciences**: Grants section is important; distinguish empirical from theoretical work
- **Engineering**: Include patents, technical reports, and industry collaborations prominently
- **Medicine/Health**: Include clinical experience and licensure

## Updating Workflow

### Continuous Updates (Do Immediately)
- New publication accepted or published
- New grant awarded
- New student begins or completes
- Award received
- Invited talk delivered

### Quarterly Review
- Check for any missed additions
- Update publication status (accepted, in press, published)
- Update student status (graduated, placed)
- Review grant status (active, completed)
- Verify all links and DOIs work

### Annual Overhaul
- Full review of all sections for accuracy and completeness
- Update research interests if focus has shifted
- Reorder sections based on current career priorities
- Archive or condense very old entries (keep but reduce detail)
- Update online profiles to match
- Generate annual activity report from CV data

### Version Control
- Maintain a master CV with everything (the "complete" version)
- Date each version (e.g., "CV_ZhangYuxin_2026-03.pdf")
- Keep a change log of what was added/modified
- Store in a version-controlled system or cloud folder with history

## Tailoring for Different Purposes

### Promotion Dossier
- Front-load research contributions (publications, grants, citations)
- Include a research impact statement
- Quantify: citation counts, h-index, grant totals
- Emphasize trajectory and growth
- Include external review letters and awards

### Job Application
- Tailor section order to match the position type (research vs. teaching emphasis)
- Match research interests to the job description
- Highlight relevant teaching experience
- Include a cover letter referencing specific CV sections

### Grant Application
- Emphasize relevant publications and preliminary data
- Highlight prior grant management experience
- Show student training and mentorship record
- Follow agency-specific format requirements exactly
- Biosketch versions: NSF, NIH, NSFC, ERC each have distinct templates

### Award Nomination
- Front-load the achievements most relevant to the award criteria
- Include quantitative impact metrics
- Highlight unique contributions to the field
- Reduce less relevant sections to save space

### Talent Program Application (人才计划)
- Emphasize representative works (代表性成果)
- Include clear narrative of research direction and vision
- Quantify impact with citations, h-index, and field ranking
- Show international collaboration and recognition
- Include both Chinese and international achievements

## Online Presence Management

### Google Scholar Profile
- Claim and verify your profile
- Merge duplicate entries
- Remove incorrectly attributed papers
- Update affiliation and research interests
- Check regularly for new citations and co-author additions

### ORCID
- Register at orcid.org (unique researcher identifier)
- Link to your publications, grants, and affiliations
- Use in all paper submissions and grant applications
- Keep updated as the authoritative source of your publication list

### ResearchGate
- Upload full-text versions of papers (respecting copyright)
- Answer questions in your area of expertise
- Track who reads and cites your work
- Connect with co-authors and collaborators

### Publish or Perish (Citation Analysis Tool)

**Publish or Perish** is a free desktop software tool that retrieves and
analyzes academic citations. Essential for CV maintenance and career development:

- **What it does:** Queries Google Scholar, Scopus, Web of Science, and
  Crossref to calculate citation metrics for any researcher or paper set.
- **Key metrics computed:** h-index, g-index, e-index, age-weighted citation
  rate (AWCR), individual h-index (hI-index), citations per paper, citations
  per author, and more.
- **Use cases for CV maintenance:**
  1. Generate a comprehensive citation report for your promotion dossier.
  2. Compare your metrics against field benchmarks.
  3. Identify your most-cited papers for "representative works" selections.
  4. Track citation growth over time for annual reports.
  5. Prepare citation summaries for talent program applications.
- **Download:** Free from harzing.com/resources/publish-or-perish
- **Tip:** Export results to CSV/BibTeX for inclusion in reports. Run the
  analysis quarterly and save snapshots to track your trajectory.

### Comprehensive Online Presence Strategy

Maintain a consistent, up-to-date presence across these platforms:

| Platform | Priority | Update Frequency | Key Action |
|----------|----------|-----------------|------------|
| ORCID | Essential | Every publication | Link all papers, grants, affiliations |
| Google Scholar | Essential | Monthly | Merge duplicates, remove errors, update affiliation |
| ResearchGate | Recommended | Quarterly | Upload preprints, answer questions |
| Personal Website | Recommended | Quarterly | Feature current projects and latest papers |
| Institutional Page | Required | Annually | Keep photo, bio, and publication list current |
| Scopus Author ID | Recommended | Check annually | Merge fragmented profiles |
| Web of Science Researcher Profile | Recommended | Check annually | Claim and verify publications |

**Google Scholar Profile Optimization:**
- Use a professional photo (increases profile click-through).
- Select 5-7 research interest keywords strategically: mix broad terms
  (for discoverability) with specific terms (for expert recognition).
- Set up citation alerts for your own papers and key competitors.
- Review the "co-authors" section for accuracy.
- Periodically search for your name variants to find unlinked papers.

**ORCID Best Practices:**
- Enable auto-update with Crossref and DataCite so new publications
  and datasets are added automatically.
- Include your ORCID iD on all paper submissions, grant applications,
  and in your email signature.
- Link your ORCID to Scopus, Web of Science, and institutional systems.
- Use ORCID as the single authoritative source for your publication record.

**ResearchGate Tips:**
- Upload author-accepted manuscripts (respecting publisher embargo periods).
- Respond to questions in your area of expertise to increase visibility.
- Track who reads and cites your work through ResearchGate analytics.
- Use ResearchGate's recommendation system to discover related work.

### Personal Academic Website
- Host a curated version of your CV
- Include downloadable papers (when copyright allows)
- Feature current projects and news
- Make contact information easy to find
- Keep updated (outdated sites create a negative impression)

### Institutional Profile
- Ensure your department page is current
- Verify publication list matches your CV
- Update photo and research description
- Include links to lab or group page

## Prompt Templates

### Generate a Complete Academic CV

```
Help me create a comprehensive academic CV:
- Name: [full name, with Chinese characters if applicable]
- Current position: [title, department, institution]
- Career stage: [assistant/associate/full professor, years since PhD]
- Field: [discipline]
- Purpose: [general use, specific application]

Information to include:
- Education: [list degrees]
- Publications: [paste publication list or provide count by type]
- Grants: [list or summarize]
- Teaching: [courses taught]
- Students supervised: [list or count]
- Awards: [list]
- Service: [editorial, review, committee roles]
- Talks: [count and key examples]

Please:
1. Organize into appropriate sections following academic CV conventions
2. Suggest optimal section ordering for my career stage and purpose
3. Format consistently throughout
4. Flag any sections that appear thin and suggest additions
5. Recommend which online profiles to prioritize
```

### Tailor CV for a Specific Application

```
Here is my full CV:
[paste CV or describe contents]

I need to tailor it for:
- Purpose: [job application, promotion, grant, award nomination, talent program]
- Target: [institution/agency/award name]
- Specific requirements: [page limits, required sections, format guidelines]
- What they value most: [research impact, teaching, service, etc.]

Please:
1. Recommend which sections to prioritize (order and detail level)
2. Suggest what to cut or condense to meet page limits
3. Identify gaps that should be addressed
4. Propose a research interests statement tailored to this opportunity
5. Format according to the specific requirements provided
```

### Create an Annual Activity Report from CV

```
Here are my activities from [year]:
[paste new CV entries or list of accomplishments]

Please generate an annual activity report covering:
1. Research output summary (publications, grants, citations)
2. Teaching summary (courses, evaluations, innovations)
3. Student supervision update (new, continuing, graduated)
4. Service contributions (editorial, review, committee)
5. Talks and presentations given
6. Awards and recognitions received
7. Year-over-year comparison metrics (if prior year data provided)
8. Goals for next year based on trajectory
```

### Update Online Academic Profiles

```
Here is my current CV:
[paste or describe]

Help me update my online presence:
1. Draft a Google Scholar research interests description (5-7 keywords)
2. Write a LinkedIn "About" section (300 words, professional but approachable)
3. Draft a personal website bio (150 words for the homepage, 500 words for the about page)
4. Create a ResearchGate research summary
5. Suggest which publications to feature on my personal website
6. Recommend a consistent set of keywords to use across all platforms
```
