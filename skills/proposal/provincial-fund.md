# Provincial Fund Proposal

## Overview

Draft proposals for provincial and municipal science and technology funds in China.
Provincial funding is a critical pillar of the Chinese research funding ecosystem,
complementing national programs (NSFC, MOST) with region-specific priorities tied to
local economic development. This skill covers the major provincial funding types,
writing strategies, and the key differences from national-level applications.

## Types of Provincial/Local Funding in China

### 1. Provincial Natural Science Foundation (省自然科学基金)

**Purpose:** Support basic and applied basic research at the provincial level.

**Subtypes:**
- General Program (面上项目): Broad support for faculty research, typically
  50,000-150,000 RMB per project over 2-3 years.
- Youth Program (青年基金): For researchers under 35 (some provinces extend to 40),
  typically 30,000-100,000 RMB.
- Excellent Young Scientists Program (优秀青年基金): For researchers under 40,
  typically 200,000-500,000 RMB, higher bar for track record.
- Key Program (重点项目): For established researchers with significant preliminary
  results, typically 500,000-1,500,000 RMB.
- Joint Fund (联合基金): Co-funded by the province and a city/enterprise/national
  fund, typically 500,000-2,000,000 RMB.

**Review process:** Similar to NSFC but with fewer reviewers (typically 3-5 mail
reviewers, sometimes a panel). Competition is less intense than NSFC but standards
are rising.

### 2. Provincial Key R&D Program (省重点研发计划)

**Purpose:** Support applied research and technology development addressing
provincial strategic needs.

**Characteristics:**
- Larger budgets: typically 500,000-5,000,000 RMB.
- Duration: 2-3 years.
- Must demonstrate clear application prospects.
- Often requires industry collaboration or matching funds.
- Topics are pre-defined by the provincial S&T department based on
  strategic priorities.

**Examples of topic areas:**
- Advanced manufacturing and intelligent equipment
- New energy vehicles and intelligent transportation
- Agricultural modernization and food safety
- Environmental protection and ecological restoration
- Information technology and artificial intelligence
- Biomedical and health technology

### 3. Science and Technology Achievement Transformation Fund (科技成果转化基金)

**Purpose:** Bridge the gap between research and commercialization.

**Characteristics:**
- Requires existing research results ready for transfer.
- Must have an industry partner or commercialization plan.
- Budget: typically 500,000-3,000,000 RMB.
- Evaluation emphasizes market potential and economic impact.
- May require matching funds from industry.

### 4. Innovation Platform and Talent Programs

- Provincial Key Lab construction (省重点实验室)
- Engineering Research Center support (工程研究中心)
- Innovation Team support (创新团队)
- Provincial talent programs (省级人才计划)
- Young talent cultivation (青年拔尖人才)

### 5. Municipal/City-Level Funding (市级科技项目)

- Generally smaller budgets than provincial programs.
- Stronger emphasis on local economic impact.
- Often require the research institution to be located in that city.
- May have industry-specific focus (e.g., automotive in Changchun, electronics
  in Shenzhen, agriculture in rural cities).

## Differences from National Funding

### Review and Competition

| Aspect | National (NSFC) | Provincial |
|--------|----------------|------------|
| Competition ratio | 15-25% for General | 25-40% typically |
| Number of reviewers | 5 mail + panel | 3-5 mail or panel |
| Review quality | Generally high | Variable by province |
| Reviewer pool | National experts | Often regional experts |
| Decision process | Highly standardized | Some administrative discretion |
| Prestige | High academic prestige | More practical weight |

### Proposal Emphasis

| Aspect | National Focus | Provincial Focus |
|--------|---------------|-----------------|
| Innovation | Fundamental scientific contribution | Applied innovation with local relevance |
| Impact | Advancing human knowledge | Economic and social development |
| Collaboration | Not required | Often required (industry/cross-sector) |
| Preliminary work | Track record and publications | Prototypes and demonstration |
| Evaluation | Academic peer review | Mixed academic and industry review |
| Outcomes | Papers, patents | Products, licenses, jobs created |

## Writing Strategies for Provincial Proposals

### Strategy 1: Align with Provincial Strategic Plans

Every province publishes strategic development plans. Your proposal must explicitly
connect to these priorities.

**How to find provincial priorities:**
- Provincial government work reports (政府工作报告)
- Provincial Five-Year Plan for S&T development (科技发展"十四五"规划)
- Provincial S&T Department announcements and call for proposals
- Provincial key industry development plans
- Visits to the S&T department website for program guidelines

**Writing approach:**
- In the first paragraph, cite the specific provincial policy your project supports.
- Use the same keywords and terminology from the policy documents.
- Explain how your project contributes to the province's competitive advantage.

### Strategy 2: Emphasize Local Industry Connection

Provincial reviewers value projects that benefit local industry.

**Tactics:**
- Name specific local companies that would benefit from your research.
- Include letters of support or intent from local enterprises.
- Describe how results will be transferred to local industry.
- Quantify potential economic impact (jobs, revenue, cost savings).
- Reference existing collaborations with local companies.

### Strategy 3: Demonstrate Regional Advantage

Explain why your institution and location are uniquely positioned for this work.

- Leverage unique local resources (geography, industry clusters, special facilities).
- Highlight your institution's strengths relevant to the project.
- Reference existing provincial labs or platforms you participate in.
- Show how this builds on previous provincial funding you have received.

### Strategy 4: Balance Academic Rigor with Practical Focus

Provincial proposals need both scientific depth and practical orientation.

**For the research content:**
- Maintain methodological rigor comparable to national proposals.
- Include clear technical innovation points.
- Provide solid preliminary data and results.

**For the application content:**
- Describe technology readiness levels and development roadmap.
- Include a technology transfer or industrialization plan.
- Define concrete deliverables (prototypes, standards, pilot demonstrations).
- Specify expected patents, software copyrights, or other IP.

### Strategy 5: Budget Appropriately

Provincial budgets are typically smaller than national ones. Adjust your scope
accordingly.

**Budget categories (typical):**
- Equipment and materials: 20-30%
- Travel and collaboration: 5-10%
- Personnel (graduate student stipends, postdoc fees): 20-30%
- Testing and analysis fees: 10-15%
- Publication and IP costs: 5-10%
- Indirect costs (管理费): 5-15% (varies by province)

**Common mistakes:**
- Proposing too ambitious a scope for the available budget.
- Not including industry matching funds when required.
- Budgeting equipment that exceeds 50% of total funding (usually capped).
- Forgetting to account for university overhead deductions.

## Province-Specific Guidelines

### Jilin Province (吉林省)

**Key programs:**
- 吉林省自然科学基金 (General, Youth, Key)
- 吉林省科技发展计划 (Key R&D, Achievement Transformation)
- 吉林省产业创新专项

**Strategic priorities:**
- Automotive and intelligent manufacturing (important for Changchun/FAW ecosystem)
- Optoelectronics and precision instruments (Changchun Institute of Optics heritage)
- Modern agriculture and black soil conservation
- Petrochemical processing (Jilin City)
- Biomedical and pharmaceutical
- Carbon peak and carbon neutrality

**Tips:**
- Strong automotive connections are valued (FAW Group, NIO, etc.).
- Reference the "振兴东北" (Northeast Revitalization) national strategy.
- Connect to Changchun's intelligent connected vehicle testing infrastructure.
- Leverage Jilin University's strengths in automotive engineering.

### Guangdong Province (广东省)

- Largest provincial science fund in China.
- General Program: up to 100,000 RMB.
- Excellent Young Scientists: 300,000 RMB.
- Key Program: up to 1,000,000 RMB.
- Strong emphasis on Pearl River Delta industry needs.
- Innovation and entrepreneurship focus.

### Other Major Provincial Programs

- **Zhejiang**: Strong in digital economy, e-commerce technology.
- **Jiangsu**: Manufacturing innovation, integration with Yangtze Delta.
- **Shanghai**: Frontier technology, international collaboration.
- **Beijing**: AI, fundamental research, national lab support.
- **Sichuan**: Electronic information, aerospace.

## Application Process Timeline

| Stage | Timing | Activities |
|-------|--------|------------|
| Call announcement | Varies by province (often Jan-Mar) | Monitor provincial S&T dept website |
| Proposal preparation | 1-2 months before deadline | Draft, internal review, get support letters |
| Institutional review | 1-2 weeks before deadline | Department and university science office review |
| Submission | By deadline | Online system submission + paper copies if required |
| Formal review | 1-2 months after deadline | Eligibility and completeness check |
| Expert review | 2-4 months | Mail review and/or panel review |
| Announcement | 3-6 months after deadline | Results published on S&T dept website |
| Contract signing | 1-2 months after announcement | Sign project agreement, receive first tranche |

## Common Pitfalls

1. **Ignoring provincial priorities**: Writing a pure basic research proposal for
   a program that values applied outcomes.
2. **Copying NSFC proposal verbatim**: Provincial reviewers expect different emphasis.
3. **No local connection**: Failing to explain relevance to the province's economy.
4. **Overly ambitious scope**: Proposing work that requires 10x the available budget.
5. **Weak team composition**: Not including industry collaborators when expected.
6. **Missing required documents**: Each province has specific attachment requirements
   (support letters, collaboration agreements, ethics approvals).

## Prompt Templates for Claude

### Draft a Provincial Natural Science Fund Proposal

```
Help me draft a proposal for [PROVINCE NAME] Provincial Natural Science Foundation
[General/Youth/Key] Program:

Research topic: [TOPIC]
Research field: [FIELD]
Budget: [AMOUNT] RMB over [DURATION] years

Key information:
- Research question: [QUESTION]
- Methodology: [APPROACH]
- Preliminary results: [WHAT YOU HAVE SO FAR]
- Innovation points: [WHAT IS NEW]
- Provincial relevance: [HOW THIS BENEFITS THE PROVINCE]
- Local industry connections: [COMPANIES, COLLABORATIONS]

Write the proposal with emphasis on:
1. Alignment with provincial strategic priorities
2. Practical application potential
3. Connection to local industry needs
4. Clear technical innovation
5. Feasible scope for the given budget
```

### Adapt an NSFC Proposal for Provincial Submission

```
I have an NSFC proposal that I want to adapt for [PROVINCE] provincial fund submission.
Help me revise it with the following changes:

Original NSFC proposal:
[PASTE KEY SECTIONS]

Provincial program: [SPECIFIC PROGRAM NAME]
Budget: [PROVINCIAL BUDGET - typically smaller]

Please:
1. Reduce scope to match the smaller budget
2. Add emphasis on provincial relevance and local industry impact
3. Strengthen the application/commercialization sections
4. Add references to provincial strategic plans
5. Adjust expected outcomes to be more practically oriented
6. Include technology transfer and industrialization plan
```

### Write Provincial Budget Justification

```
Create a detailed budget justification for a provincial fund proposal:

Total budget: [AMOUNT] RMB
Duration: [YEARS] years
Province: [PROVINCE NAME]

Project activities requiring funding:
[LIST KEY ACTIVITIES]

Team composition:
[LIST TEAM MEMBERS AND ROLES]

Generate a line-item budget with justification for each category,
following typical provincial fund guidelines. Ensure equipment does not
exceed budget caps and include appropriate overhead rates.
```
