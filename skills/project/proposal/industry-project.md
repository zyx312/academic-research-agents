# Industry Project Proposal

## Overview

Draft proposals for industry-funded research projects (横向课题). Industry projects are
a vital part of academic research in China and globally, providing funding, real-world
problem access, and technology transfer opportunities. This skill covers the full
lifecycle from proposal through contract negotiation, deliverable management, and
project closure.

## Types of Industry Projects

### 1. Commissioned Research and Development (委托研发)

The company defines a specific technical problem and commissions the university
to develop a solution.

**Characteristics:**
- Clear problem statement from the company
- Defined deliverables (prototype, algorithm, report)
- IP typically belongs to the commissioning company or is shared
- Budget: 100,000-5,000,000+ RMB depending on scope
- Duration: 6 months to 3 years

### 2. Joint Development (合作开发/联合研发)

Both parties contribute resources and expertise to develop technology together.

**Characteristics:**
- Shared problem definition and approach design
- Both parties contribute personnel, equipment, or data
- IP is typically co-owned with defined licensing terms
- Often leads to longer-term partnerships
- May involve seconding researchers to company labs

### 3. Technical Consulting (技术咨询/技术服务)

The university provides expert analysis, testing, or advisory services.

**Characteristics:**
- Shorter duration (weeks to months)
- Deliverable is usually a report, analysis, or recommendation
- Lower budget: 50,000-500,000 RMB typically
- IP is usually not involved (consulting output, not invention)
- May involve laboratory testing or certification

### 4. Technology Transfer and Licensing (技术转让/技术许可)

Transfer of existing university IP to a company for commercialization.

**Characteristics:**
- Based on existing patents, software, or know-how
- Payment may be lump sum, royalties, or equity
- University TTO typically leads the negotiation
- May include a transition period with technical support

### 5. Industry-Sponsored Graduate Students

Companies fund graduate students working on topics relevant to their business.

**Characteristics:**
- Company provides tuition, stipend, or project funding
- Student works on a topic of mutual interest
- Thesis results may be subject to confidentiality agreements
- Student may intern at the company

## Proposal Structure for Industry Clients

Industry proposals differ significantly from academic grant proposals. Industry
clients want to know: What will you deliver? When? How much?

### Section 1: Executive Summary (1 page)

- State the client's problem in their language (not academic jargon).
- Summarize your proposed approach in 2-3 sentences.
- Highlight key deliverables and timeline.
- State the total budget.
- Emphasize your unique qualifications.

### Section 2: Problem Statement and Background

- Restate the client's challenge, demonstrating deep understanding.
- Describe current limitations of available solutions.
- Connect the problem to broader industry trends.
- Reference any preliminary discussions or site visits.
- Use industry terminology, not pure academic language.

### Section 3: Technical Approach

- Describe your proposed methodology clearly.
- Break the work into defined phases (typically 3-5).
- Explain the rationale for your approach.
- Identify key technical risks and mitigation strategies.
- Reference your relevant prior work and publications.
- Include preliminary results or proof-of-concept data if available.

### Section 4: Deliverables

Define each deliverable precisely:

| Deliverable | Description | Format | Due Date |
|-------------|-------------|--------|----------|
| D1 | Literature survey and state-of-art analysis | PDF report (30+ pages) | Month 3 |
| D2 | Algorithm design and simulation results | Software + report | Month 6 |
| D3 | Prototype implementation | Working system + documentation | Month 9 |
| D4 | Testing and validation results | Test report with data | Month 11 |
| D5 | Final report and technology transfer | Report + training session | Month 12 |

### Section 5: Timeline and Milestones

Use a Gantt chart or milestone table:

| Phase | Tasks | Duration | Start | End | Milestone |
|-------|-------|----------|-------|-----|-----------|
| Phase 1 | Requirements and survey | 3 months | M1 | M3 | Requirements doc signed off |
| Phase 2 | Design and development | 4 months | M3 | M7 | Prototype demo |
| Phase 3 | Testing and optimization | 3 months | M7 | M10 | Test report delivered |
| Phase 4 | Documentation and transfer | 2 months | M10 | M12 | Final acceptance |

### Section 6: Team Qualifications

- PI profile: relevant expertise, publications, prior industry projects.
- Team members: roles and relevant experience.
- Facilities and equipment available.
- Prior collaborations with industry (especially the client or their competitors).
- Successful technology transfer examples.

### Section 7: Budget

Industry budgets should be transparent and justified:

| Category | Amount (RMB) | Justification |
|----------|-------------|---------------|
| Personnel | XXX | PI (X months), Postdoc (Y months), 2 grad students |
| Equipment | XXX | Specific items needed for the project |
| Materials and consumables | XXX | Testing materials, cloud computing, etc. |
| Travel | XXX | X trips to client site for meetings/testing |
| Testing and analysis | XXX | External lab testing, instrument time |
| Indirect costs (管理费) | XXX | University overhead (typically 10-20%) |
| **Total** | **XXX** | |

## Contract Negotiation Points

### IP Ownership (知识产权归属)

This is the most critical and contentious issue. Common arrangements:

1. **Company owns all IP**: Appropriate for commissioned research where the company
   defines the problem and pays fully. University may retain right to publish
   (after review/delay).

2. **Joint ownership**: Common for joint development. Requires clear terms on:
   - Who can license to third parties?
   - Who handles prosecution and costs?
   - Revenue sharing from licensing.

3. **University owns, company gets exclusive license**: University retains IP;
   company gets exclusive commercial rights in defined field/territory.

4. **Background IP vs. foreground IP**: Pre-existing IP (background) remains with
   the originator. New IP created during the project (foreground) follows the
   agreed arrangement.

**Recommendation:** Clearly separate background IP from foreground IP in the contract.
The university should never assign pre-existing IP to a company as part of a project
agreement.

### Confidentiality (保密条款)

- Define what information is confidential.
- Set the confidentiality period (typically 3-5 years).
- Allow exceptions for: prior knowledge, public information, independently developed
  information, legally required disclosures.
- Specify handling of confidential data (secure storage, access control, destruction).

### Publication Rights (发表权)

- University researchers need to publish for career advancement.
- Negotiate a review period (typically 30-90 days) during which the company can
  review manuscripts before submission.
- The company may request removal of proprietary information but should not
  indefinitely block publication.
- Patent filing should take priority -- agree that the university will not
  publish before a patent application is filed.

### Liability and Warranties

- University provides results "as-is" -- not a product guarantee.
- Limit liability to the amount of the contract.
- Do not warrant commercial success or fitness for a particular purpose.
- Include force majeure provisions.

### Termination Clauses

- Define conditions for early termination by either party.
- Specify how IP, data, and materials are handled upon termination.
- Define payment obligations for work completed before termination.

## Deliverable Management

### Setting Expectations

- Define acceptance criteria for each deliverable upfront.
- Include a review and feedback period (typically 2-4 weeks).
- Specify the format and level of detail expected.
- Agree on who signs off on deliverable acceptance.

### Progress Reporting

Maintain regular communication with the client:
- Monthly progress emails or brief reports.
- Quarterly milestone review meetings.
- Immediate notification of any risks, delays, or scope changes.
- Demo sessions for prototype deliverables.

### Common Deliverable Formats

- **Technical reports**: PDF with clear structure (executive summary, methodology,
  results, conclusions, references).
- **Software deliverables**: Source code + documentation + user guide + test results.
- **Hardware prototypes**: Physical system + technical specifications + operating manual.
- **Data deliverables**: Datasets + metadata + data dictionary + access instructions.
- **Training**: Presentation materials + hands-on session + Q&A documentation.

## Billing Milestones

### Typical Payment Structure

| Milestone | Payment (% of total) | Trigger |
|-----------|---------------------|---------|
| Contract signing | 30-40% | Signed contract |
| Mid-term review | 20-30% | Milestone report accepted |
| Final delivery | 20-30% | Final deliverables accepted |
| Acceptance | 10-20% | Final acceptance sign-off |

### Invoice Management

- Submit invoices promptly after milestone completion.
- Include the contract number and milestone reference.
- Follow the university's financial procedures for horizontal project billing
  (横向课题开票流程).
- Track payment receipt and follow up on overdue payments.

## Managing Industry vs. Academic Expectations

### Common Tensions

| Industry Expects | Academic Norm | Resolution |
|------------------|--------------|------------|
| Working product | Research prototype | Define "prototype" clearly in contract |
| Quick results | Thorough investigation | Set realistic milestones with buffers |
| Proprietary secrecy | Open publication | Negotiate review period before publication |
| Dedicated team | Shared attention | Be honest about effort allocation |
| Guaranteed outcomes | Best-effort research | Frame deliverables as "research toward X" |
| Regular meetings | Autonomy | Agree on reporting cadence upfront |

### Best Practices

1. **Underpromise, overdeliver**: Set conservative milestones and exceed them.
2. **Communicate proactively**: Bad news early is better than bad news late.
3. **Document everything**: Written records prevent misunderstandings.
4. **Manage scope creep**: If the client adds requirements, negotiate additional
   time/budget formally (签订补充协议).
5. **Maintain academic standards**: Industry funding should not compromise research
   integrity or publication ethics.

## China-Specific Considerations

### University Financial Procedures

- Horizontal projects (横向课题) go through the university's science and technology
  department (科技处) and finance department (财务处).
- The university typically charges an overhead (管理费) of 5-20% depending on
  institutional policy.
- PI usually has more spending discretion with horizontal funds than with
  vertical (government) funds.
- Tax invoices (增值税发票) are issued by the university.

### Contract Registration

- All industry contracts must be registered with the university's S&T office.
- The contract must comply with the university's横向课题管理办法.
- Some universities require internal approval before signing.

### Personal Income Considerations

- PI may receive a portion of horizontal project funds as labor compensation
  (劳务费), subject to personal income tax.
- The proportion and tax treatment vary by university policy.

## Prompt Templates for Claude

### Draft an Industry Project Proposal

```
Draft a technical proposal for an industry-funded project:

Client company: [COMPANY NAME]
Industry sector: [SECTOR]
Technical problem: [DESCRIPTION OF THE PROBLEM]
Our relevant expertise: [TEAM STRENGTHS]
Proposed approach: [HIGH-LEVEL METHODOLOGY]
Duration: [MONTHS]
Budget range: [AMOUNT RMB]

Include these sections:
1. Executive summary (1 page, written for industry readers)
2. Problem statement (in industry language)
3. Technical approach (3-5 phases)
4. Deliverables table with specific outputs and dates
5. Timeline with milestones
6. Team qualifications
7. Budget breakdown

Tone: professional, practical, outcome-focused. Minimize academic jargon.
```

### Draft IP and Confidentiality Terms

```
Draft IP and confidentiality clauses for a university-industry contract:

Project type: [commissioned research / joint development / consulting]
Company: [NAME]
University: [NAME]
Key IP considerations:
- Background IP brought by university: [DESCRIPTION]
- Background IP brought by company: [DESCRIPTION]
- Expected foreground IP: [PATENTS, SOFTWARE, DATA]
- Publication needs: [YES/NO, with what delay]

Generate contract language (in Chinese if specified) covering:
1. IP ownership for foreground IP
2. Background IP protection
3. Confidentiality obligations and duration
4. Publication review rights and timeline
5. Licensing terms if applicable
```

### Convert Academic Research into Industry Proposal

```
I have academic research results that I want to propose as an industry project.
Help me convert the following academic content into an industry-focused proposal:

Academic research summary:
[PASTE RESEARCH DESCRIPTION]

Target industry application: [APPLICATION]
Target company type: [DESCRIPTION]

Rewrite emphasizing:
1. Business value and ROI for the company
2. Concrete deliverables rather than academic contributions
3. Timeline with practical milestones
4. Risk mitigation strategies
5. Clear cost-benefit proposition
```
