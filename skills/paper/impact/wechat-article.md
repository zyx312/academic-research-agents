# WeChat Article Writing Skill

## Overview

This skill guides academics in writing effective WeChat Official Account (微信公众号) articles to disseminate research findings, build scholarly reputation, and engage broader audiences. It covers content strategy, writing techniques for non-specialist readers, visual design, and audience growth.

## Why WeChat for Academics

WeChat Official Accounts remain the dominant platform for knowledge sharing in Chinese academic and professional communities. Benefits include:

- Reaching industry practitioners, policymakers, and the public beyond journal readership
- Building name recognition that supports grant applications and talent program evaluations
- Creating a permanent, searchable archive of accessible research summaries
- Networking with peers who share and comment on your content
- Demonstrating broader impact (社会服务) for institutional evaluations
- Attracting prospective graduate students who discover your work

## Content Strategy

### Content Pillars (Choose 3-4 for Consistency)

1. **Research Insights** (研究解读)
   - Accessible summaries of your own published papers
   - Key findings translated into practical implications
   - Behind-the-scenes research stories and methodology insights

2. **Field Commentary** (领域评论)
   - Analysis of trending topics in your field
   - Interpretation of new policies, standards, or regulations
   - Commentary on breakthrough papers from other groups
   - Conference highlights and emerging research directions

3. **Tutorials and Guides** (教程指南)
   - How-to guides for research methods, tools, or techniques
   - Graduate student advice (writing, publishing, career planning)
   - Software tutorials, data analysis walkthroughs
   - Literature review strategies

4. **Personal Stories** (学术生活)
   - Lab culture and team achievements
   - Conference travel reflections
   - Career reflections and lessons learned
   - Student graduation celebrations and achievements

5. **Industry Bridge** (产学研)
   - How research applies to industry problems
   - Technology transfer stories
   - Collaboration opportunities and project calls

### Content Calendar

Recommended posting frequency: 1-2 articles per week for active growth, minimum 2 per month for maintenance.

Monthly planning template:
- Week 1: Research insight (your latest paper or finding)
- Week 2: Field commentary (respond to a timely topic)
- Week 3: Tutorial or guide (evergreen, shareable content)
- Week 4: Personal/team story or industry connection

## Article Structure for Non-Specialist Readers

### The Inverted Pyramid for Academic Content

1. **Hook** (First 3 lines visible in preview): The most compelling finding or question
2. **Context**: Why this matters to the reader (real-world connection)
3. **Core Content**: The main findings, methods, or arguments (simplified)
4. **Evidence**: Supporting data, figures, or examples
5. **Implications**: What this means for the reader, industry, or society
6. **Call to Action**: What the reader should do next

### Writing Principles

**Simplify without dumbing down:**
- Replace jargon with plain language or provide immediate explanations
- Use analogies and metaphors to explain complex concepts
- One main idea per article (resist the urge to cover everything)
- Write sentences under 25 characters where possible for mobile readability

**Storytelling techniques:**
- Start with a question, problem, or surprising fact (not "Recently, our team...")
- Use concrete examples before abstract principles
- Create narrative tension: problem, investigation, discovery, implication
- Include human elements: who did the research, why they cared, what surprised them

**Reader-first thinking:**
- Answer "So what?" and "Why should I care?" early in the article
- Anticipate reader questions and address them in order
- End with something actionable or thought-provoking
- Write for the "interested non-expert" (a smart person in a different field)

### Optimal Article Length

- Short-form updates: 800-1,200 characters (quick insights, news reactions)
- Standard articles: 2,000-3,500 characters (research summaries, commentary)
- Deep dives: 4,000-6,000 characters (tutorials, comprehensive reviews)
- Mobile reading sweet spot: 3-5 minute read time (approximately 2,500-3,500 characters)

## Headline Formulas

### High-Performing Patterns

1. **Question format**: "为什么[topic]正在改变[field]?"
2. **Number list**: "[N]个关于[topic]的关键发现"
3. **Contrast**: "[Topic A]vs[Topic B]: 哪个更[adjective]?"
4. **How-to**: "如何用[method]解决[problem]"
5. **News angle**: "[Journal/Conference]最新: [brief finding]"
6. **Myth-busting**: "关于[topic]的[N]个常见误解"
7. **Achievement**: "我们的最新研究登上[journal]: [one-line finding]"
8. **Trend analysis**: "[Year]年[field]的[N]大趋势"

### Headline Do's and Don'ts

Do:
- Use specific numbers and concrete details
- Create curiosity gaps (promise information the reader wants)
- Include keywords for searchability
- Keep under 30 characters for full mobile display

Don't:
- Use clickbait that the article cannot deliver on
- Be vague ("A Study on Something")
- Use excessive punctuation or emoji in the title
- Make claims stronger than the evidence supports

## Visual Design

### Image Strategy
- **Cover image**: High-quality, relevant, 900x383 px for single-image display
- **In-article images**: Diagrams, charts, infographics that summarize key points
- **Original figures**: Redraw key paper figures for clarity at mobile resolution
- **Photos**: Lab scenes, conference photos, team pictures for personal articles
- Always include alt-text descriptions for accessibility

### Design Tools
- Canva or similar tools for infographics and cover images
- 135editor, Xiumi (秀米), or similar WeChat formatting tools
- Draw.io or PowerPoint for simplified research diagrams
- High-resolution screenshots of key paper figures (with proper attribution)

### Formatting for Mobile Reading
- Use section headers (bold text as dividers)
- Keep paragraphs short (2-3 sentences)
- Use numbered or bulleted lists for key points
- Add whitespace between sections
- Use color and bold text sparingly for emphasis
- Include a visual element every 300-500 characters to break up text

## Engagement and Growth

### Building Follower Base
- Cross-promote articles on personal WeChat Moments
- Share in relevant WeChat groups (academic, industry, alumni)
- Add QR code to conference presentations and posters
- Include public account information in email signatures
- Collaborate with other academic accounts for cross-promotion
- Encourage lab members to share articles in their networks

### Engagement Metrics to Track
- Article reads (阅读数): Total views within 7 days
- Shares (分享数): Times shared to Moments or groups
- Favorites (收藏数): Saved for later reading
- Comments (留言数): Reader engagement depth
- Follower growth rate: New followers per article
- Read-to-share ratio: Indicates content quality and shareability

### Posting Schedule Optimization
- Best posting times: 7:00-9:00 AM (commute), 12:00-1:00 PM (lunch), 8:00-10:00 PM (evening)
- Weekday articles generally outperform weekend posts for academic content
- Avoid posting during major holidays unless content is holiday-themed
- Consistency matters more than optimal timing

### Community Building
- Respond to comments promptly and thoughtfully
- Ask questions at the end of articles to encourage discussion
- Feature reader questions in follow-up articles
- Create a reader community group for dedicated followers
- Run occasional Q&A sessions or polls

## Research Visualization with Mind Maps

### Using Mind Maps for Article Illustration

Mind maps are highly effective for WeChat articles because they compress
complex research structures into a single, visually appealing image that
mobile readers can quickly absorb. They work particularly well for:
- Summarizing a paper's contribution structure
- Mapping the landscape of a research field
- Illustrating relationships between concepts
- Creating taxonomy visualizations for review articles

### Generating Mind Maps with Mermaid

Mermaid is a text-based diagramming tool that can generate mind maps directly
from structured text. This is particularly useful when combined with AI
tools like gpt_academic, which can automatically extract a paper's structure
and generate Mermaid code.

**Basic Mermaid Mind Map Syntax:**
```
mindmap
  root((Research Topic))
    Branch 1
      Sub-topic 1a
      Sub-topic 1b
    Branch 2
      Sub-topic 2a
      Sub-topic 2b
    Branch 3
      Sub-topic 3a
```

**Example: Generating a Research Paper Mind Map**

For a paper on "Multi-Sensor Fusion for SOTIF":
```
mindmap
  root((Multi-Sensor Fusion for SOTIF))
    Perception Challenges
      Sensor degradation in adverse weather
      Cross-sensor inconsistency
      Unknown object categories
    Proposed Framework
      Confidence-aware fusion
      Uncertainty quantification
      Graceful degradation strategy
    Validation
      KITTI benchmark results
      Adverse weather dataset
      Real vehicle testing
    Key Contributions
      Novel fusion architecture
      SOTIF-aware uncertainty model
      Open-source implementation
```

**Workflow for WeChat Articles:**
1. Extract the paper's key concepts and relationships.
2. Write the Mermaid mind map code (or use gpt_academic to generate it automatically).
3. Render the Mermaid code using:
   - Mermaid Live Editor (mermaid.live) for quick visualization
   - VS Code with Mermaid extension for local rendering
   - gpt_academic's built-in Mermaid rendering capability
4. Export as a high-resolution PNG or SVG image.
5. Embed the image in your WeChat article as a visual summary.

**Tips for Mind Map Quality:**
- Limit to 3-4 main branches for readability on mobile screens.
- Use concise labels (under 20 characters per node).
- Export at high resolution (at least 1200px wide) for mobile clarity.
- Place the mind map near the beginning of the article as an overview,
  or at the end as a summary.

### Other Visualization Tools for WeChat Articles

- **XMind**: Professional mind mapping software with attractive export options
- **Draw.io / diagrams.net**: Free diagram tool for flowcharts and system architectures
- **Canva**: Design tool with templates for research infographics
- **ProcessOn (processon.com)**: Chinese-language diagramming platform popular in academia
- **Excalidraw**: Hand-drawn style diagrams that feel approachable and informal

## Legal and Ethical Considerations

- Respect copyright: do not reproduce full paper text; summarize and cite
- Obtain co-author permission before publicizing joint research
- Clearly distinguish peer-reviewed findings from opinions and speculation
- Disclose funding sources and potential conflicts of interest
- Follow journal embargo policies for pre-publication publicity
- Respect institutional communication policies

## Prompt Templates

### Write a Research Summary Article

```
Write a WeChat article summarizing this research paper:
- Paper title: [title]
- Journal: [name], published [date]
- Key findings: [brief summary of main results]
- Target audience: [academics in the field / industry professionals / general public]
- Tone: [accessible and engaging / semi-technical / formal]

The article should:
1. Open with a compelling hook (question, surprising fact, or real-world problem)
2. Explain the research context in 2-3 sentences without jargon
3. Present the key findings using analogies or concrete examples
4. Include 2-3 potential implications for the reader
5. End with a thought-provoking question or call to action
6. Be approximately [2000-3500] characters in Chinese
7. Suggest a headline using one of the high-performing patterns
8. Recommend 2-3 images or diagrams to include
```

### Write a Field Commentary Article

```
Write a WeChat commentary article on:
- Topic: [current event, new policy, breakthrough paper, trend]
- My perspective/angle: [your unique viewpoint]
- Target audience: [who should read this]
- Key points to make: [2-3 main arguments]

The article should:
1. Start with the news or trigger event
2. Provide necessary context for readers unfamiliar with the background
3. Present a clear, well-reasoned analysis
4. Include both the opportunities and challenges
5. Connect to broader trends in the field
6. End with forward-looking insights or predictions
7. Maintain a balanced, authoritative tone
8. Be approximately [2500-4000] characters in Chinese
```
