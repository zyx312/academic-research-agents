# Cover Letter for Journal Submission

## Purpose

This skill provides templates, frameworks, and best practices for writing cover letters that accompany manuscript submissions to academic journals. A well-crafted cover letter helps the editor understand the significance of your work and makes a positive first impression that can influence whether your manuscript is sent out for peer review.

## Why Cover Letters Matter

The cover letter is your first communication with the editor. It serves to:

1. **Frame your work**: Explain significance beyond what the abstract conveys
2. **Demonstrate fit**: Show you understand the journal's scope and audience
3. **Guide editor decisions**: Help the editor identify appropriate reviewers
4. **Address logistics**: Confirm compliance with journal policies
5. **Make a professional impression**: Signal that you are a serious, careful researcher

Some journals explicitly require cover letters; others make them optional. Always include one -- it never hurts and often helps.

## Cover Letter Structure

### Complete Template

```
[Your Name]
[Your Title and Affiliation]
[Department, University/Institution]
[Address]
[Email]
[Date]

[Editor Name], [Title]
Editor-in-Chief / Associate Editor
[Journal Full Name]

Dear [Dr./Prof. Editor Last Name],

[PARAGRAPH 1: MANUSCRIPT INTRODUCTION]
We are pleased to submit our manuscript entitled "[Manuscript Title]"
for consideration for publication in [Journal Name] as a [article type:
Original Research Article / Review Article / Short Communication / etc.].
This manuscript has [X] authors and is [X] pages in length, containing
[X] figures and [X] tables.

[PARAGRAPH 2: KEY FINDINGS AND SIGNIFICANCE]
[Describe the problem addressed, the approach taken, and the key findings
in 3-5 sentences. Do NOT copy the abstract. Write in plain language that
conveys why this work matters. Include your most impressive quantitative
result.]

[PARAGRAPH 3: NOVELTY AND CONTRIBUTION]
[Explain what is genuinely new about this work. State how it advances
the field beyond existing literature. Be specific: "To the best of our
knowledge, this is the first study to..." or "Unlike previous approaches,
our method..."]

[PARAGRAPH 4: FIT WITH JOURNAL SCOPE]
[Explain why this journal is the right venue. Reference specific topics
the journal covers, recent papers the journal has published on related
topics, or the journal's stated scope. Show you have done your homework.]

[PARAGRAPH 5: SUGGESTED REVIEWERS]
We suggest the following potential reviewers who have relevant expertise:
1. [Name], [Affiliation], [Email] - Expertise in [area]
2. [Name], [Affiliation], [Email] - Expertise in [area]
3. [Name], [Affiliation], [Email] - Expertise in [area]

[PARAGRAPH 6: EXCLUDED REVIEWERS (if applicable)]
We respectfully request that [Name(s)] not be invited to review this
manuscript due to [brief, professional reason: competing research interest /
recent collaboration / known disagreement on methodology].

[PARAGRAPH 7: DECLARATIONS]
We confirm that:
- This manuscript has not been published previously and is not under
  consideration at any other journal.
- All authors have read and approved the submitted version.
- All authors have agreed to the submission to [Journal Name].
- [If applicable: This work extends our conference paper [citation],
  with approximately X% new content including...]
- [If applicable: Data and code are available at [URL/DOI].]
- [If applicable: Ethics approval was obtained from [IRB/Ethics Board].]
- [If applicable: All funding sources are declared in the manuscript.]

[PARAGRAPH 8: CONFLICTS OF INTEREST]
[The authors declare no conflicts of interest. / The authors declare
the following potential conflicts of interest: ...]

We look forward to hearing from you at your earliest convenience.

Sincerely,

[Corresponding Author Name]
[Title, Affiliation]
[Email]
[ORCID: xxxx-xxxx-xxxx-xxxx]

On behalf of all authors:
[List all author names]
```

## Section-by-Section Guidance

### Paragraph 1: Manuscript Introduction
- State the manuscript title exactly as it appears on the paper
- Specify the article type (check journal categories)
- Mention the number of authors, pages, figures, tables, supplementary files
- Keep it factual and brief: 2-3 sentences maximum

### Paragraph 2: Key Findings
This is the most important paragraph. Write it to be compelling but honest.

**Do**:
- Describe the problem in one sentence that a non-specialist editor can understand
- State your main finding with a quantitative result
- Explain the practical or theoretical significance
- Use strong, active verbs: "We demonstrate..." "Our results reveal..."

**Do NOT**:
- Copy-paste your abstract
- Use excessive jargon or acronyms
- Oversell with superlatives ("groundbreaking," "revolutionary")
- Be vague ("interesting results," "promising approach")

### Paragraph 3: Novelty Statement
This is where you differentiate from prior work. Be specific:

**Strong examples**:
- "To our knowledge, this is the first study to combine [method A] with [approach B] for [application C]."
- "Unlike previous approaches that rely on [limitation], our method achieves [advantage] by [innovation]."
- "We provide the first large-scale empirical evaluation of [topic] across [number] scenarios."

**Weak examples** (avoid):
- "Our work is novel and significant."
- "This has not been done before." (without specifying what "this" is)
- "We improve on existing methods." (without saying how or by how much)

### Paragraph 4: Journal Fit
Show the editor you chose this journal deliberately:

- Reference the journal's scope statement from its website
- Mention 2-3 recent papers published in this journal on related topics
- Explain how your audience aligns with the journal's readership
- If the journal has a special issue on your topic, mention it

**Example**: "We believe this manuscript aligns well with [Journal Name]'s focus on [scope area], as demonstrated by recent publications such as [Author (Year)] and [Author (Year)] on related topics."

### Paragraph 5: Suggesting Reviewers

**How many**: Most journals request 3-6 suggested reviewers.

**Selection criteria**:
- Choose researchers who are active in your specific sub-field
- Select from different institutions and countries for diversity
- Avoid anyone from your own institution or recent collaborators (past 3 years)
- Include a mix of senior and mid-career researchers
- Ensure they have published recently (last 3 years) on related topics

**What to include for each**:
- Full name and title
- Current affiliation
- Active email address (verify it works)
- Brief note on their relevant expertise

**Strategic considerations**:
- Reviewers who are likely fair and knowledgeable, not just likely to accept
- Reviewers who have cited or been cited by your work (they know the context)
- Avoid suggesting your thesis advisor, close friends, or frequent co-authors

### Paragraph 6: Excluding Reviewers

You may exclude reviewers for legitimate reasons:
- Direct competitors who may delay or unfairly review your work
- Researchers with whom you have documented disputes
- Recent collaborators (conflict of interest)

**How to phrase it**:
- "Due to a competing research interest" (professional, no drama)
- "Due to a recent collaboration that may constitute a conflict of interest"
- Do NOT write: "They will reject our paper" or "They disagree with our approach"
- Keep it to 1-3 excluded reviewers maximum

### Paragraph 7: Declarations

Include all that apply:
- **Originality**: Not published or under consideration elsewhere
- **Author approval**: All authors approved the submission
- **Prior publication**: Disclose any conference paper, preprint, or related publication
- **Data availability**: Where data/code can be accessed
- **Ethics**: IRB approval, informed consent, animal welfare compliance
- **Funding**: Funding sources (also listed in Acknowledgments)
- **AI tools**: Disclosure of AI assistance if required by journal policy

### Paragraph 8: Conflicts of Interest
- Declare any financial or non-financial conflicts
- If none: "The authors declare no conflicts of interest"
- When in doubt, disclose -- transparency is always better

## Cover Letters for Different Scenarios

### Resubmission After Rejection (to a Different Journal)
```
We are pleased to submit our manuscript "[Title]" for consideration
in [New Journal]. This manuscript was previously submitted to
[Previous Journal], where it received constructive feedback. We have
substantially revised the manuscript based on the reviewers' comments,
including [briefly list major changes]. We believe the revised
manuscript is well-suited for [New Journal]'s readership because
[reason for fit].
```

### Revision Resubmission (to the Same Journal)
```
We are pleased to resubmit our revised manuscript "[Title]"
(Manuscript ID: [ID]) for consideration in [Journal Name]. We
sincerely thank the editor and reviewers for their constructive
comments, which have significantly improved the manuscript.

We have addressed all reviewer comments point-by-point in the
attached response letter. Key changes include:
1. [Major change 1]
2. [Major change 2]
3. [Major change 3]

All changes are highlighted in the tracked-changes version of
the manuscript.
```

### Extended Conference Paper
```
We wish to disclose that this manuscript extends our conference
paper published at [Conference Name, Year] [citation]. The journal
version contains approximately [X]% new content, including:
- [New contribution 1: e.g., extended methodology with formal proofs]
- [New contribution 2: e.g., additional experiments on datasets X and Y]
- [New contribution 3: e.g., comprehensive ablation study]

The conference paper is cited as Reference [N] in the manuscript.
```

### Special Issue Submission
```
We are pleased to submit our manuscript "[Title]" for the Special
Issue on "[Special Issue Title]" in [Journal Name], guest-edited
by [Editor Names]. Our work directly addresses the call for papers
theme of [specific theme] by [brief description of how].
```

### Invited Submission
```
We are pleased to submit our manuscript "[Title]" for [Journal Name],
following the kind invitation from [Editor Name] after our
presentation at [Conference/Event Name] on [Date]. As discussed,
this manuscript presents [brief description].
```

## Format and Tone Guidelines

### Length
- Keep the cover letter to ONE page (single-spaced)
- Editors read many cover letters -- be concise
- If you cannot fit everything, cut the journal-fit paragraph before cutting the novelty paragraph

### Tone
- **Professional and confident**, not arrogant or apologetic
- Use "we" consistently (even for single-author papers in many fields)
- Avoid excessive hedging: "We believe" is fine, "We humbly think perhaps" is not
- Do not criticize other researchers or competing work

### Formatting
- Use standard business letter format
- Single-spaced, normal margins, readable font (11-12pt)
- Include date
- Sign with corresponding author's name and ORCID

## Common Mistakes

1. **Copying the abstract**: The cover letter should add context beyond the abstract
2. **Being too long**: One page maximum; editors are busy
3. **Forgetting the journal name**: Triple-check you have the correct journal throughout
4. **Generic letters**: "We submit to your prestigious journal" without specifics
5. **Not suggesting reviewers**: Miss an opportunity to guide the review process
6. **Suggesting inappropriate reviewers**: Close collaborators, thesis advisors, or friends
7. **Missing declarations**: Forgetting to state originality, conflicts, or prior publication
8. **Wrong editor name**: Research who the current editor is -- editors change
9. **Excessive self-promotion**: Let the work speak; avoid "revolutionary" claims
10. **Forgetting AI disclosure**: Many journals now require disclosure of AI tool usage

## Journal Selection Tools

Choosing the right journal is critical. Use these tools to evaluate fit, impact, and acceptance likelihood before writing your cover letter.

### Journal Selection Platforms

| Tool | URL | Features |
|------|-----|----------|
| **Journal Citation Reports (JCR)** | jcr.clarivate.com | Official Impact Factor (IF) rankings by Clarivate. Filter by category, quartile (Q1-Q4), and subject area. Requires institutional access. |
| **LetPub** | letpub.com.cn/index.php?page=journalapp | Chinese researcher's go-to for journal selection. Shows IF, acceptance rate, review speed, and user-submitted experiences (审稿周期). Supports filtering by SCI/EI zone (中科院分区). |
| **小木虫 (Emuch)** | emuch.net | China's largest academic forum. Search for specific journal experiences: review timeline, reviewer comments, acceptance difficulty. Invaluable for understanding real-world journal behavior. |
| **Scopus Sources** | scopus.com/sources | Browse journals indexed in Scopus. Shows CiteScore, SJR, SNIP metrics. Useful for verifying Scopus indexing status. |
| **DOAJ** | doaj.org | Directory of Open Access Journals. Verify if an OA journal is legitimate (not predatory). |
| **Journal Finder (Elsevier)** | journalfinder.elsevier.com | Paste your abstract; get journal recommendations ranked by relevance. Elsevier journals only. |
| **Journal Suggester (Springer)** | journalsuggester.springer.com | Same concept for Springer Nature journals. |
| **IEEE Publication Recommender** | publication-recommender.ieee.org | Recommend IEEE journals/conferences based on your abstract. |
| **Beall's List** | beallslist.net | List of potentially predatory publishers and journals. Check before submitting to unfamiliar journals. |

### Chinese Academy of Sciences (CAS) Journal Ranking (中科院分区)

The CAS ranking system is widely used in Chinese universities for faculty evaluation and graduation requirements:

| Zone | Meaning | Approximate IF Range | Evaluation Weight |
|------|---------|---------------------|-------------------|
| **1区 (Zone 1)** | Top 5% by IF in each category | Highest in category | Highest recognition |
| **2区 (Zone 2)** | Top 6-20% | Upper range | Strong recognition |
| **3区 (Zone 3)** | Top 21-50% | Mid range | Moderate recognition |
| **4区 (Zone 4)** | Bottom 50% | Lower range | Basic recognition |

**Note**: CAS zones and JCR quartiles (Q1-Q4) use different calculation methods and may not align perfectly. Always check both.

### Journal Selection Decision Framework

When choosing a journal, evaluate these factors:

1. **Scope fit**: Does your paper match the journal's aims and scope? Read 3-5 recent papers.
2. **Impact Factor / CAS Zone**: Match to your institutional requirements.
3. **Review speed**: Check LetPub or 小木虫 for reported review timelines.
4. **Acceptance rate**: Lower rates generally correlate with higher prestige.
5. **Open Access policy**: Consider APC costs and funder requirements.
6. **Indexing**: Verify SCI, EI, Scopus, or CSCD indexing as needed.
7. **Page charges**: Some journals charge per page or for color figures.
8. **Audience**: Who reads this journal? Will your target audience find your paper?

## Pre-Submission Checklist

- [ ] Cover letter addressed to the correct editor by name
- [ ] Journal name is correct throughout (not leftover from a previous submission)
- [ ] Manuscript title matches exactly between cover letter and paper
- [ ] Article type specified (original research, review, etc.)
- [ ] Key findings described in your own words (not copied from abstract)
- [ ] Novelty clearly stated
- [ ] Journal fit explained with specific references
- [ ] 3-6 suggested reviewers with affiliations and emails
- [ ] Excluded reviewers listed with professional reasons (if any)
- [ ] All required declarations included
- [ ] Cover letter is under one page
- [ ] Proofread for grammar and spelling
- [ ] Co-authors have reviewed and approved the cover letter
- [ ] AI usage disclosed if required by journal policy

## Prompt Templates for Claude

### Draft a Cover Letter
```
I am submitting a manuscript to [JOURNAL NAME].

Manuscript details:
- Title: [TITLE]
- Authors: [AUTHOR LIST]
- Article type: [ORIGINAL RESEARCH / REVIEW / SHORT COMMUNICATION]
- Pages: [X], Figures: [X], Tables: [X]

Key findings:
[DESCRIBE YOUR MAIN RESULTS IN 3-5 BULLET POINTS]

Novelty:
[WHAT IS NEW ABOUT THIS WORK COMPARED TO EXISTING LITERATURE]

Why this journal:
[WHY YOU CHOSE THIS JOURNAL]

Please draft a professional cover letter that:
1. Introduces the manuscript concisely
2. Highlights significance without copying the abstract
3. States the novelty clearly
4. Explains fit with the journal
5. Includes placeholders for suggested reviewers
6. Includes all standard declarations
7. Stays within one page
```

### Suggest Appropriate Reviewers
```
My paper is on [TOPIC] and will be submitted to [JOURNAL].
Key methods/areas: [LIST METHODS AND AREAS]

The following researchers should NOT be suggested (co-authors, collaborators, same institution):
[LIST NAMES TO EXCLUDE]

Please suggest 5 potential reviewers by identifying:
1. Researchers who have published on [TOPIC] in the last 3 years
2. Their affiliation and relevant expertise
3. Why they would be appropriate for this manuscript

Note: I will verify their contact information independently.
```

### Adapt Cover Letter for Resubmission
```
My paper was rejected from [PREVIOUS JOURNAL]. I am now submitting to [NEW JOURNAL].

The key reviewer concerns were:
[LIST MAIN CONCERNS]

Changes I made in response:
[LIST CHANGES]

Please help me draft a cover letter for [NEW JOURNAL] that:
1. Acknowledges the paper was previously submitted elsewhere (without naming the journal)
2. Highlights the improvements made
3. Explains fit with the new journal's scope
4. Maintains a positive, forward-looking tone
```

### Write Revision Cover Letter
```
I received a [MAJOR / MINOR] revision decision for my paper at [JOURNAL].
Manuscript ID: [ID]

The editor requested:
[EDITOR COMMENTS]

I have addressed the following:
[LIST OF MAJOR CHANGES MADE]

Please draft a revision cover letter that:
1. Thanks the editor and reviewers
2. Summarizes the key changes made
3. References the detailed point-by-point response (attached separately)
4. Expresses confidence in the revised manuscript
5. Maintains a professional, grateful tone
```

### Disclose Conference Paper Extension
```
My journal manuscript extends a conference paper:
- Conference paper: [TITLE, CONFERENCE, YEAR]
- Journal manuscript title: [TITLE]

New content in the journal version:
[LIST NEW CONTRIBUTIONS WITH APPROXIMATE PERCENTAGES]

Please draft a disclosure paragraph for my cover letter that:
1. Transparently identifies the conference paper
2. Quantifies the new content
3. Lists specific additions
4. Demonstrates the journal version is a substantial extension
```
